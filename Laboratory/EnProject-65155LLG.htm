METHODICAL APPROACH TO AN ESTIMATE OF THE NET PRIMARY PRODUCTION RESPONSE TO CHANGES IN CLIMATE FOR THE EUROPEAN RUSSIA.

Project 99–05–65155 of the Russian Fund of Basic Research,
leader Dr. Leonid L. Golubyatnikov

Project dates        1999–2000

Information

PossiAimed at developing a methodology for quantitative prediction of the net primary production response to changes in climate. Modern approaches to modelling this production characteristic of the vegetation cover for terrestrial ecosystems are considered. Model estimations of the net primary production for zonal vegetation of the European Russia are obtained. The methods based on empirical relationships between climate and net primary production (regression-based models) and on the models simulating the mechanical transfer of carbon (combined with nitrogen and water into some models) among various pools in ecosystems (process-based models) are used. The model estimations of the net primary production are compared with empirical data. A technique is presented to forecast changes in the net primary production as a function of the radiation balance and annual evapotranspiration values. The heterogeneous of the vegetation cover at the local territory (Kurskaya oblast) for modern climate is analyzed. Dynamic of the surface phytomass density within the season relative leaves' square and albedo on the three scale levels are presented. Biological mechanisms of the heterogeneous changes of the territory under the same parameters (density of the surface phytomass, relative leaves' square and albedo) and interconnection of the reflecting and structural characteristics is shown.
