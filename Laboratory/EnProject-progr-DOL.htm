Current Publication

 

ZONAL VEGETATION SUCCESSIONS AND SHIFT OF THE BIOCLIMATIC ZONES UNDER EXTREMAL AND CATASTROPHICAL EFFECTS

Project of the basic research program of the Presidium of the Russion Academy of Sciences "Changes in the Natural Environment and Climate: Natural Disasters",
leader Prof. Dmitrii O. Logofet

Project dates        2003–2004

Information

This project is an attempt to apply previously formulated general principles of modelling vegetation dynamics taking into account climate changes (Logofet et al., 1997; Logofet, 1999; Logofet, Denisenko, 1999) to analysis of the long-term vegetation dynamics in the forest-steppe zone of the European part of Russia and its forecast under special climate change scenarios. The work is founded on the original technique for designing Markov chains as vegetation succession models suggested and developed in a number of previous projects and systematically described with an example of broad-leaved forest (Logofet, Lesnaya, 2000; Logofet, 2002; Logofet, Korotkov, 2002). Geobotanical knowledge summarized in a scheme of successional transitions and estimations of average duration for certain succession stages appears as a starting point for the model creation. In those cases, where the knowledge connects average durations and/or transition directions with certain climate-induced environmental factors, the possibiity appears to model a temporal succession dynamics depending on the given scenario of key factors' change. Formally the model represents a random chain of the Markov type (or non-Markov type) with finite numbers of states and discrete transition time according to the given scheme. The modelling results in distributions of relative areas for succession stages at any time moment within accepted scenario, as well as estimations for the attainment times of various vegetation states over the territory under study.
