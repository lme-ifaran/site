Current Publication

 

STUDY OF THE INTERACTION FOR CLIMATE CHANGE AND THE METHANE FLUXES IN CRYOLITOZONE ECOSYSTEMS OF THE WESTERN SIBERIA ON THE BASIS OF EXPERIMENTAL AND MODELLING DATA

Project 12-05-33050 of the Russian Fund of Basic Research
leader Dr. Maxim M. Arzhanov

Project dates        2012–2013

Information

2012:
Coupled scheme of the carbon cycle and the processes of heat and moisture transport in the soil is developed by the participants of the project. The scheme is validated and intensity of methane emission by wetlands in the XX century is calculated. Database of initial values and input parameters for the models of carbon cycle and processes of heat and moisture transport in the soil was created. Regional methane flux from the surface of floodplain lakes in the tundra zone of Western Siberia on the basis of the field measurements was analyzed by the participants of the project. The estimated methane flux from the lake surface to the atmosphere in the warm season in the Arctic tundra is found to be about 0.5 KtCH4, in typical tundra 2.9 KtCH4, in southern tundra 8.5 KtCH4. Possible changes in the main characteristics of the permafrost zone of the region under consideration by the end of the XXI century under various scenarios of anthropogenic impact are estimated. The increase of the depth of the seasonal thawing permafrost layer in Western Siberia was found. Permafrost regime change from seasonal thawing to seasonal freezing is expected in the southern part of the Western Siberia by the end of the XXI century.
