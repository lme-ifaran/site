COUPLED LOW-PARAMETERIC MODEL OF CLIMATE AND CARBON CYCLE FOR THEIR REGIONAL AND GLOBAL CHANGES ESTIMATION

Project 05–05–65137 of the Russian Fund of Basic Research,
leader Dr. Alexandr S. Ginzburg

Project dates        2005–2007

Information

During the project period it was calculated radiative forcing and temperature changes due to essential raise of CO2 and CH4 concentration within the frame of radiative-convective climate model. It was investigated the stability and relaxation process related to pre-industrial carbon balance in the terrestrial climate system and possibility of fast methane warming for 55 million years ago were made using simple climatic models.

In year of 2005 it was investigated the temporal changes of CO2 and CH4 concentrations in the urban atmosphere and especially in the Moscow urban area using TROICA and MSU observation data.

The demonstration version of radiative-convective climate model (RCM) created in 2005 later was improved with implementation of the line-by-line solar and thermal radiation fluxes calculation developed in Kurchatov Institute.

During 2006-07 it was analyzed so called Global warming potential and other emission metrics using in the IPPC reports for different greenhouse gases influence comparison. It was shown the limitation of this approach.

The results of numerical modeling of vertical temperature profile changes under the different СО2 и СН4 concentration increasing show that developed Equilibrium global warming potential (EGWP) for СН4 is the same as "classical" GWR at 100 year time horizon and EGWP could be used for any greenhouse gases concentration and for any time horizons.

Using the simplest energy balance climate model used in IPPC reports it was shows that for 5°C global warming 55 million years ago during Paleocene-Eocene Thermal Maximum (PETM) 250 Gt CH4 was needed to be in the atmosphere with annually emission in 25 Gt, if methane live time is about 10 years as now. High methane concentration changes the atmospheric chemistry and leads to its longer live time up to few decades and annually emission could be less intensive (less than 10 Gt).

During the first stage of the project it was developed and validated basic model of global carbon cycle with 4 boxes - land biota, atmosphere, ocean and lithosphere. In 2007 it developed the family of global carbon cycle simple models consists from three to six boxes.

It was shown that pre-industrial carbon balance is stable. It means that if anthropogenic carbon emission is stopped the Earth biogeochemical system will come back to initial balance.

The number and stability of stationary states in this set of models are shown to be kept but the dynamics of carbon reservoirs essentially depends on calibration of flows responsible for the matter exchange between atmosphere and terrestrial biota.

Relaxation times to the stable pre-industrial equilibrium for carbon storage values in the basic model are investigated under perturbation of this equilibrium without an anthropogenic input. However, the pre-industrial equilibrium is not stable relatively to permanently acting anthropogenic perturbation. Another steady state in the model, more hypothetical than real, can be characterized by disappearing the terrestrial biota compartment. Numeric simulation of the system trajectories under the modern value of anthropogenic flux shows that the system becomes unstable and a carbon contents in the atmosphere grows up to approximately 1100 GtC and then slightly going to the stable state during several centuries. If emission continues up to the end of fossil fuel reserves, the system does not converge to a new steady state. Emission of carbon to the atmosphere is compensated by its stock to terrestrial biota and oceans.

Within the project framework some additional investigations and publication took place, based on previous project investigators results:
- written, published and presented at 15th session of UN Commission on Sustainable Development the UN Foundation and SigmaXi Report "Confronting Climate Change: Avoiding the unmanageable and managing the unavoidable" 150 pp. (A. Ginzburg - leading author of the report);
- a series of low-parametric dynamic models of carbon cycle in bog ecosystems is created together with data from certain territories for its calibration and verification (N. Zavalishin);
- re-calibrating of TsuBiMo model with eddy-covariance measurements at Takayama was made (G. Alexandrov);
- the hypnosis that weather abnormalities during autumn and winter 2006-2007 create a very special condition for methane concentration growing was presented (E. Fedorova, A. Ginzburg, A. Vinogradova).
