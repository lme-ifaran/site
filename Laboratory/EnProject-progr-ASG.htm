STUDY OF THE IMPACT OF ANTHROPOGENIC ENERGY AND TROPOSPHERIC ANTHROPOGENIC AEROSOL ON THE CLIMATE PROCESSES

Project of the basic research program of the Earth Sciences Department of the Russion Academy of Sciences "Hydrospheric and Atmospheric Processes: Formation, Change and Regulation of Earth's Climate",
leader Dr. Alexandr S. Ginzburg

Project dates        2006–2008

Information

The project goal: Estimating the role of anthropogenic energy fluxes at the industrial and urbanized territories in regional and global climatic processes. Comparative analysis of natural and technological aerosol effects and analysis of possible aerosol impact on the atmospheric and climatic processes. Foundation: Powerful aerosol patterns in the atmosphere can appear as a result of natural and anthropogenic processes such as volcanic explosions, desertification, forest fires, as well as technological issues, primarily on urbanized territories. Comparative analysis of climate effects induced by different aerosols is necessary for estimating a risk of purposeful aerosol impact on climate. Anthropogenic energy fluxes, especially on urbanized territories, are to become a factor of essential importance affecting regional and even global climatic processes. At present a number of modern climate models start to take into account and parameterize these fluxes in chracteristics of climate state and dynamics. Study of the influence of energy fluxes on urban lands on the city heat island formation in different climatic zones is projected. In 2006 impact of carbon-black and sulfur aerosols on the thermal regime of the atmosphere and underlying surface will be calculated. In 2007-2008 impact of dust and volcanic aerosols on climatic processes with green-house gases concentration increase is supposed to be carried out.
