ANTROPHOGENIC IMPACTS ON THE INTERACTION BETWEEN CLIMATE AND TERRESTRIAL ECOSYSTEMS

Project of the basic research program of the Earth Sciences Department of the Russion Academy of Sciences "Hydrospheric and Atmospheric Processes: Formation, Change and Regulation of Earth's Climate",
leader Dr. Leonid L. Golubyatnikov

Project dates        2006–2008

Information

The project is aimed at the study of climate and environment changes as well as analysis of climate-biota interactions. The basic task is to estimate an intraction of the climate change and zonal ecosystems under anthropogenic perturbations using mathematical models. Improper predictability of the climate change consequences and insufficient knowledge on principles of vegetation distribution over the Earth surface in dependence on climate initiate problems in reliable forecasting these changes. Changes in plant communities distribution directly impacts on such climatic parameters as albedo, height of roughness, quantity of water vapour in the atmosphere etc. Qualitative and numerical estimations for the impact of human-induced changes in the structure, functioning and spatial distribution of the vegetation in zonal ecosystems on climatic characteristics are to be carried out. Besides, terrestrial ecosystems are able to uptake a part of anthropogenic carbon dioxide emissions slowing down global warming. In turn, this process of absorption depends on the atmospheric carbon dioxide concentration and state of the climate system. Special attention in the project will be payed to the biotic regulation mechanism of the climate change. Solutions for the problems stated above are to be founded on the coupling of the climate model of intermediate complexity and dynamic vegetation distribution model developed in IAP RAS. Analysis of the joint dynamics of climate and spatial distribution of zonal ecosystems will allow to obtain more accurate estimations of vegetation control properties in the climate-biota system.
