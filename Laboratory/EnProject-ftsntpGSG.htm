MATTER FLUXES AND ENERGY EXCHANGE BETWEEN GEOSPHERES INCLUDE LITOSPHERE, OCEAN AND ATMOSPHERE

The State Scientific Program,
leader Academician Georgii S. Golitsyn

Project dates        2002–2004

Information

In 2002-2004 geological and geophysical field studies have been carried out in the region of Nothern Caucasus and Elbrus volcanic center in order to estimate energy and matter fluxes (oil series, primarily methane) from terrestrial and underwater volcanic explosions. Estimations for matter fluxes in oceanic water column and its boundaries with atmosphere and lithosphere were made during expeditions of scientific ships to Arctic, Antarctic and Atlantic ocean (Institute of oceanology). Attention was paid to the role of biosphere in a formation of matter fluxes from land to ocean. An experimental study of vertical fluxes of methane and other GHG in the Western Siberia (Great Vasyugan Wetlands) was aimed to estimate deposit of the wetland territories in the global GHG flux on the boundary atmosphere-lithosphere. Comparative analysis of vertical methane fluxes by mathematical models of the Institute of geosphere dynamics and State Hydrological Observatory showed a contradictive character of experimental data and conclusions on the global processes forming the methane atmospheric balance. In 2003 the complicated experiment on a detailed research of gas exchange processes (for carbon dioxide and methane) in the system "atmosphere-water surface" started at the lake Baikal coast. The data obtained confirm the role of the lake-river system in the formation of GHG global balance. A biogeochemical model of intergeospheric carbon exchange accounting for the lake-river system has been designed at the Institute of atmospheric optics (Siberian Branch of RAS). In 2002-2003 experimental studies of vertical fluxes of atmospheric small gas components near the Earth surface were made. Estimated is an impact of vegetation on the dry admixture deposition. Energy exchange between atmosphere and ocean under rapidly developing surface air convection is theoretically studied provided significant difference of air and underlying surface temperatures. Role of large world city agglomerations as energy-active zones of interaction between geopheres is analysed.
