LARGE-SCALE ENERGETIC AND LANDSCAPE FACTORS OF VEGETATION DIVERSITY

Venevsky S., Venevskaia I.
Ecology Letters, 2003, Vol. 6, pp. 1004-1016

Аннотация

We show that at large scale the species number of vascular plants can be predicted to а major extent Ъу chmatically determined latent heat for evaporation and geometrical structure of landscape, described as an altitudinal difference. Application of the energy- equivalence rule across plant communities for transpiration per area unit, and use of fractal theory for the description of habitat occupation by vegetation results in а physically based species energy rdationship. Application of averaged global constants in the relationship results in а species – area equation with known parameters. Despite its simple form, this species – energy relationship generally reproduces global patterns of vegetation diversity at two scales – 10 000 and 100 000 km2 – and is applicable for different regions across scales from 100 to million km . The proposed theory produces more robust results compared with the correlated-based approaches, which link plant diversity patterns with climate variables, and allows the inclusion of an evolutionary component. The final chmate-richness equation for vascular plants has а simple and consistent analytical form and requires easily measurable variables.
