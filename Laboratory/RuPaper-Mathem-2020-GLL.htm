REALISTIC CHOICE OF ANNUAL MATRICES CONTRACTS THE RANGE OF Lambdas ESTIMATES

Logofet D.O., Golubyatnikov L.L. , Ulanova N.G.

Mathematics, 2020, vol.8(12), 2252

Аннотация

In matrix population modeling the multi-year monitoring of a population structure results in a set of annual population projection matrices (PPMs), which gives rise to the stochastic growth rate lambdas, a quantitative measure of long-term population viability. This measure is usually found in the paradigm of population growth in a variable environment. The environment is represented by the set of PPMs, and lambdas ensues from a long sequence of PPMs chosen at random from the given set. because the known rules of random choice, such as the iid (independent and identically distributed) matrices, are generally artificial, the challenge is to find a more realistic rule. We achieve this with the a following a Markov chain that models, in a certain sense, the real variations in the environment. We develop a novel method to construct the ruling Markov chain from long-term weather data and to simulate, in a Monte Carlo mode, the long sequences of PPMs resulting in the estimates of lambdas. The stochastic nature of sequences causes the estimates to vary within some range, and we compare the range obtained by the "realistic choice" from 10 PPMs for a local population of a Red-Book species to those using the iid choice. As noted in the title of this paper, this realistic choice contracts the range of lambdas estimates, thus improving the estimation and confirming the Red-Book status of the species.
