Current Publication

 

MATHEMATICAL MODELING OF THE TRANSFORMATION OF LANDSCAPES IN THE PERMAFROST REGION WITH THE USAGE OF THE PALEOCLIMATIC DATA

Project 06-05-64502 of the Russian Fund of Basic Research,
leader Dr. Pavel F. Demchenko

Project dates        2006–2008

Information

A module for thermal amd hydrological processes in soil suitable for climate models of intermediate complexity is developed. Employing this module, changes in characteristics of thermal and hydrological state of the soil are computed for 1960-2000 forced by the reanalysis data. In this, the modelled soil active layer thicknesses are in agreement with station data. Changes in Siberian river discharges in the second half of the 20th century are estimated. Climate model developed at IAP RAS (IAP RAS CM) is extended by a module for thermal processes in soil and by a module for methane cycle. This module, in particular, takes into account a response of wetlands methane emission on climate changes. With this mode, numerical experiments for the 17th-21st centuries are performed which are forced by greenhouse gases and tropospheric sulphates. The IAP RAS CM as a whole reproduces preindustrial and present day characteristics of seasonal thaw/freezing. In the 21st century, permafrost extent is diminished by 4 mln sq km. Spatial reconstructions of the northern Eurasian landscapes for the late part of the Last Glacial Maximum are prepared. The Alleroed warming resulted in a formation of polizonality when southward of tundra and forested tundra either dark needleleaf forests (at the western part) or light needleleaf forests (at the eastern part) were formed. Cryolitozne was limited in this period. The Late Drias cooling returned vegetation hyperzonality with dominating either tundra or steppe landscapes and extensive cryolitozone. Spatial reconstructions of the northern Eurasian cryolitozone starting from the Preboreal and ending at the Holocene Optimum (10-5 kyr before present) are prepared. It is shown that, early in the current interglacial, most drastic degradation of cryolitozone was in the eastern part of the continent. However, near the Holocene Optimum, most dramatic shrink of permafrost was in the western and central part of the domain.
