ATMOSPHERIC GHG BALANCE OVER THE RUSSIAN TERRITORY ASSESSMENT: A PROTOTYPE OF GEOINFORMATION SYSTEM (GIS) DEVELOPMENT AND CREATION

Project 05–05–08045_ofi of the Russian Fund of Basic Research,
leader Dr. Alexandr S. Ginzburg

Project dates        2005–2006

Information

The main purpose of the project is to create and develop a technology of estimating atmospheric balance of green-house gases on the Russian territory taking into account natural and anthropogenic sources and stocks as well as carbon dioxide and methane transport. Such estimations are necessary for more accurate account of Russian national interests during the Kioto Protocol realization. Using as a basis atmospheric studies carried out by IAP and other institutes of RAS and Federal Service for Hydrometeorology and Environmental Monitoring, a prototype of modern geoinformatic system is to be created allowing to estimate a role of Russian territory in the global climate change. Another basic tools are climate models of intermediate complexity, models of atmospheric transport with heterogeneity of underlying surface and models of biogeochemical cycles. The project follows the main aim of the "Knowledge generation" part of the Federal Programme "Research and Innovations in Scientific and Technological priorities" for 2002-2006 formulated as «implementation of problem-oriented basic investigations and applied studies of advanced level». The suggested technology of estimating a potential of the territory of Russian Federation in regulating anthropogenic climate change will be founded on the modern global climate models as well as regional models of atmospheric processes and biogeochemical cycles of green-house gases. The project results will be used as an important part in the analytic information network system GEOSINet - future segment of the information virtual environment based on GRID technologies. Potential users and customers for the GIS-prototype are governmental structures of Russian Federation, state and private organizations responsible for the achievement of the Action Plan for the Kyoto Protocol realization in Russia.
