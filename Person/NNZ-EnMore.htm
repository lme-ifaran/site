Nikolay N. Zavalishin

Born December 04, 1972 in Moscow, Russia. After graduating from the school in 1990 I entered the Moscow Institute of electronics and mathematics (MIEM) and completed its Faculty of Applied Mathematics in 1996 with the university diploma in mathematical modelling.  
Although this specialty at the Department of mathematical modelling was oriented to methods of analytical and continuum mechanics, ecological problems and ecosystems modeling issues became the most interest at the time of finishing studies. This was the reason of my activities in mathematical ecology started during the diploma preparation and continued since 1996 when I became a post-graduate student at the Laboratory of mathematical ecology in the A.M. Obukhov Institute of atmospheric physics of Russian Academy of Sciences under supervising of Prof. D.O. Logofet. All the professional background in ecosystem theory, ecological modeling and mathematical methods of nonlinear dynamics was received in this Laboratory.  
In 1999-2000 I participated in the project on the design and development of global vegetation models and their reaction to Climate Change at the Potsdam Institute of Climate Impact Research (project leader Prof. Y.M. Svirezhev). Mathematical models of matter and energy cycles in local bog ecosystems of flow-reservoir type under climatic and human perturbations were the subject of my PhD thesis prepared in 1999-2002 and defended at the Dorodnitsyn Computer Centre of Russian Academy of Sciences in 2002. Simultaneously in 1995 – 2003 I worked as a software developer at the Locomotive Engine Design Bureau of Russian Ministry of Railways raising my background in computer programming and software development since a realization of any mathematical model often depends on programming skills of its authors. But the most valuable for me are many wonderful co-workers and friends there. (site PKBCT)

Since 2003 I am a researcher at the Laboratory of mathematical ecology at the A.M. Obukhov Institute of atmospheric physics RAS. My main research areas are general ecology problems, ecosystem models, nonlinear population dynamics, global and local biogeochemical cycles, vegetation dynamics under the Climate Change and anthropogenic perturbations, regular and chaotic oscillations in ecological and physical models.
